# TooXTips — User Persona

> Single-user application. There is one persona: Franklin (the owner and sole user).

---

## Franklin Ouattara — The User

**Role:** Personal user, developer, owner  
**Device:** Android mobile (primary), iOS (secondary)  
**Usage pattern:** Daily, throughout the day — morning planning, session logging during the day, AI check-ins in the evening

---

### Goals

- **Time management:** Know what's happening today and this week at a glance, without opening multiple apps
- **Pursue long-term goals:** Track progress on Wills (things like "learn Japanese", "get better at chess") through sessions with notes, so there's a record of effort over time
- **Spending awareness:** Know where money is going without manually reviewing bank statements (v1.1)
- **AI as a thinking partner:** Get proactive suggestions from an AI that has full context — agenda, ongoing wills, recent sessions — not just a generic chatbot

---

### Pain points the app solves

| Pain point | How TooXTips addresses it |
|---|---|
| Agenda and goals live in separate apps — no connection | Single app where an agenda event can be linked to a Will |
| Goal tracking apps lose context between sessions | Session notes are stored and fed to the AI for continuity |
| AI assistants don't know my schedule or habits | AI context includes today's agenda, recent sessions, and unanchored events |
| Proactive reminders are too generic | 8pm daily notification is generated by the AI based on actual data |
| Expense tracking requires manual categorisation in external apps | (v1.1) Built-in expense logging with AI-powered analysis |

---

### What Franklin expects from the AI

- To feel like a personal chief of staff, not a chatbot
- To reference what was actually done ("you practised chess twice this week")
- To suggest realistic next steps based on the agenda
- To stay useful even offline (graceful degradation, not a broken screen)
- To never expose private data externally beyond the Claude API call

---

### Feature validation rule

Every feature decision must answer: **does this make Franklin's daily life measurably simpler or more intentional?**  
If not, it belongs in the backlog, not the MVP.
